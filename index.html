About

Teresa He is a pianist and composer whose artistry bridges the worlds of performance and creation. Born in Beijing, she began piano studies before the age of four and joined Dr. Wenli Zhou’s studio in 2014, developing her musical voice in the Greater Houston area. Before college, she performed recitals and chamber music in Beijing and Dalian.

In 2016, Teresa began her Bachelor of Music in piano performance at the University of Texas at Austin, studying under Prof. David Renner and Prof. Andrew Brownell. Passionate about chamber music, she founded the Pastel Piano Trio in her freshman year, working with the Miró Quartet and pianist Patti Wolf.

Alongside her performance career, Teresa has cultivated a distinctive compositional voice shaped not only by her musical influences—impressionism, lyrical romanticism, and contemporary soundscapes—but also by her background in public affairs. Drawing on her studies and work in international development, political reform, and the ideals of liberty and democracy, she explores themes of resilience, human connection, and societal transformation in her works. Her compositions, including the Piano Quintet No. 1 in C Minor and Prelude for Piano and Violoncello – April, have been praised for their emotional depth, coloristic textures, and ability to weave narratives that resonate across cultures.
